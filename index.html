About

I design and build things that actually run. Most of my projects start in CAD, move through a 3D printer and a soldering iron, and end as something I can hold or drive — a competition robot, a remote-controlled prop, a board that moves its own pieces. I compete in VEX Robotics and spend the rest of my time on personal builds that let me practice mechanical design, embedded electronics, and the firmware in between. I like problems that don't fit neatly into one field.

PRJ-0022025—26

Remote-Controlled Dalek

4-motor drivetrain · custom mounts · ESP32 control

A drivable Dalek prop built from the ground up: a four-motor drivetrain on custom 3D-printed mounts, driven by an Arduino Nano ESP32 generating PWM into separate motor-driver ICs, with a buck-converter power stage off a switched 12 V supply. Schematic designed in KiCad.

ESP32KiCad PWM motor control3D printing Power electronics
PRJ-0032025

Automated Magnetic Chessboard

Hall-effect sensing · shift registers · piece tracking

A chessboard that senses and moves its own pieces: a grid of Hall-effect sensors read through shift registers detects piece positions, paired with a magnet mechanism beneath the board to relocate them. A tight mix of sensing, multiplexing, and mechanical design.

Hall sensorsShift registers Embedded CMechanism design

"The Cruelest Years" — Tabletop Wargame

Original WWII game design · rules · production

An original WWII tabletop wargame, from systems and balance through to a fully produced rulebook and annexes. I laid out the rules as print-ready half-letter booklets for saddle-stitch printing — game design plus the unglamorous work of shipping something playable.

Game designSystems Rules writingPrint production

iOS App Switcher Gesture Proposal

Interaction design · submitted to Apple

A proposed gesture for the iOS app switcher — swipe down to send an app to the back of the stack — submitted through Apple's Feedback Assistant. I built an interactive web prototype with spring physics to demonstrate exactly how the motion would feel.
